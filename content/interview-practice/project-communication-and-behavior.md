# Process & Requirements

1. **When a client changes scope mid-project, do you push back immediately, go along and adjust silently, or negotiate a new plan first?**

I discuss the change with the client first to understand the new requirements, assess the impact on time and cost, and agree on an updated plan before proceeding. Clear expectations help avoid surprises.

1. **Do you prefer detailed upfront specs, or figuring it out as you go?**

I prefer having clear requirements upfront because it reduces misunderstandings. That said, I'm comfortable working with evolving requirements and adapting as the project progresses.

1. **When stuck, do you ask the client fast, dig for a solution solo, or research broadly before deciding?**

I first investigate the issue myself using documentation and reliable resources. If it affects the project or requires a business decision, I reach out to the client with the issue and my recommended solution.

1. **Do you check in with clients proactively, or mainly when milestones are hit?**

I communicate proactively. I provide regular progress updates, especially if there are important milestones, risks, or decisions that require the client's input. I believe consistent communication builds trust.

# Communication Style

1. **A client hasn't replied in 3 days and a decision is blocking your work. What do you do?**

I usually tried to communicate with client proactively. if hasn't replied in 3 days, i wouldn't just wait , First I'd send a polite follow-up summarizing the pending decision, explain how it's affecting the timeline, ask for clarification, in the mean time i will continue others task that has no blocking and prepare alternative solutions, goal is to keep project moving while maintain clear communication

1. **You disagree with a client's technical decision. Do you say so directly, suggest alternatives gently, or defer to their call?**

I explain my recommendation with clear reasoning and suggest alternatives, but I respect the client's final decision after we've discussed the trade-offs.

# Handling Ambiguity & Structure

1. **You get a brief with no clear deliverable format. Do you ask for specifics before starting, or produce a draft and let them react to it?**

I clarify the key requirements first, then create a draft if needed so the client can review and provide feedback early.

1. **Do you prefer working from a detailed project plan, or a rough direction you fill in as you go?**

I prefer starting with clear project plan because it helps align expectation, , priorities, and timelines. That said, project requirement may often change, especially in Agile environments. Once I understand the goal, I can break the work into manageable tasks and adapt as requirements evolve, and refining the plan as we learn more.

# Pace & Prioritization

1. **You have two deadlines colliding. How do you decide what goes first?**

For Upwork: I plan my workload carefully to avoid conflict, I evaluate which one one is more time sensitive or business critical, communicate any risks early, and ensure both clients stay informed throughout the process.

Normally: I prioritize based on urgency, business impact, and agreed deadlines. If two deadlines conflict, I communicate with both clients early, set clear expectations, and focus on the task with the highest priority or closest deadline. I believe proactive communication is the key to delivering quality work.

1. **Under time pressure, do you cut scope, extend the timeline, or push through with less sleep/more hours?**

I prioritize the critical work first, communicate early if adjustments are needed, and I'm willing to put in extra hours when necessary—but I don't sacrifice quality or rely on working without sleep.

# Problem-Solving

1. **You hit a technical blocker you've never seen before. Do you troubleshoot solo for a while first, ask a peer/community immediately, or go straight to the client with the issue?**

I troubleshoot independently first. If the issue persists, I use reliable resources or ask for expert input. If it impacts the project or timeline, I update the client promptly with the issue and my proposed next steps.

# Accountability & Mistakes

1. **You realize you made an error that's already been delivered to the client. What's your first move?**

I inform the client as soon as I confirm the issue, explain what happened, and present a clear plan to fix it. If possible, I'll correct the error immediately and verify everything before sending the updated version. I believe being transparent and proactive builds trust.

1. **A project is falling behind schedule. Do you flag it early, or wait until you have a fix to bring the news?**

I flag it as soon as I identify a real risk to the timeline. I don't like surprising clients. When I reach out, I explain the reason, the impact, and the steps I'm taking to get the project back on track, along with any options if adjustments are needed.

# Collaboration

1. **On a team project, do you prefer clearly assigned lanes, or fluid overlap where anyone jumps in wherever needed?**

I prefer clear clearly assigned lanes/ownership so everyone knows who's responsible for what, but I'm also happy to jump in and help when priorities shift or a teammate needs support.

1. **When giving feedback on someone else's work, are you direct and specific, or diplomatic and softened?**

I believe feedback should be honest, specific, and constructive. I focus on the work, not the person, explain the reasoning behind my suggestions, and offer practical improvements. My goal is to help achieve the best outcome while maintaining a positive working relationship.
